Selling on Homemade

Beginner's Guide to Selling on Homemade

Interested in selling on Homemade? Welcome!

This guide helps you through the first steps of opening your shop, shares tips on how to get a strong start, and provides resources for further guidance along the way.

Get to know Homemade

Homemade is a unique marketplace for authentic homemade cuisines, handmade items, vintage goods, and handicraft. On Homemade, you’ll find a community of homechefs, makers, bakers, small-business owners, and shoppers who all share a passion for uncommon and rare goods.

If you’re new to Homemade, take some time to get to know us. Review our Partner policies and learn about fees for selling on Homemade to make sure that selling on Homemade is right for you and your business.

Open your Account

Before you sell your items, sign in or create an Homemade account. After creating your account, add your profile picture and bio to let other people in the Homemade community know who you are.

To open your Homemadeaccount:

  • Open the Homemade app on your mobile device
  • Tap Sign Up
  • Select ‘Food’ or ‘Non-Food’
  • Fill out the registration form with the required details.
  • Tap Confirm
Do I need a business license to sell on Homemade?

Homemade doesn’t require partners to have a business license to sell on Homemade.

However, when selling on Homemade you're required to follow any laws that apply to you as a small business selling online. If you have any questions regarding which laws apply to you, please contact a qualified professional.

Also, the law mandates FSSAI registration be done for all homemade food selling partners. However, a FSSAI license is required only when the annual turnover is above Rs 12 Lakh.

Finish your setup

Once you’ve signed up to sell on Homemade, there are a few steps you need to take and prepare to start selling items. Like any online business, you should think through key factors in your selling workflow. Consider things like production time, policies, and your packaging for shipping.

Add listings to your profile

Once you’ve completed all your profile sections, you are ready to start adding items. When you add an item to your Homemade profile, we call this a listing.

Manage your profile

After you’ve listed your items in your profile, there are other factors to manage as your business develops and grows.

Tips for managing:

  • Make sure you follow best practices for customer service.
  • Learn what to do when you make your first sale.
  • Toggle the ‘Accepting Order’ option in your account to take a break when needed.
What Can I Sell?

Homemade is a platform to help creators like you. Here, we bring together all the home chefs, artist, artisans and food smiths on-board. Together, we thrive to be the largest community anywhere on the globe to offer homemade products only. Our quality assurance team ensures that on this platform we feature products that are exclusively prepared at home.

All items sold on Homemade must also adhere to our Prohibited Items Policy.

Prohibited Items

We prohibit certain types of items which present legal risks to our community; others are inconsistent with our values, are harmful to our members, or simply are not in the spirit of Homemade.

Following types of items are prohibited from being sold on Homemade:

  • Food items and non-food products processed in mass-production facilities
  • Alcohol, tobacco, drugs, drug paraphernalia, and medical drugs
  • Certain animal products and human remains
  • Dangerous items, including hazardous materials, recalled items, and weapons
  • Hate items, including items that promote, support, or glorify hatred
  • Illegal items, items promoting illegal activity, and highly regulated items
  • Internationally regulated items
  • Pornography and mature content
  • Violent items, including items that promote, support, or glorify violence

Shipping basics

Shipping is one of the most important factors customers look at when deciding whether to make a purchase. For HomemadePartners, creating an effective and competitive shipping strategy is a key part of running your online business.

The first step in creating your shipping strategy is to go over all your shipping logistics. You're responsible for ensuring orders are sent to buyers.

When creating a shipping strategy:

  • Consider what shipping timelines will work well for you, your products, and your customers.
  • Create a workflow with expectations that you can easily meet, even during high volume times. This includes setting realistic processing times for your items.
  • Gather shipping materials, like packaging and boxes or envelopes.
  • Set aside time regularly to fulfill orders, manage shipments, and update your Homemade listings.
